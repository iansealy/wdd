WDD2 Reading List

Required reading for this module

  • Donald A Norman - The Design of Everyday Things
    The classic text explains the cognitive aspects of everyday design and fundamental principles of good usability in a straightforward and entertaining way, dotted with examples and case studies to bring it all alive.
  • Russ Unger, Carolyn Chandler - A Project Guide to UX Design
    This is the core text for this module. Ross and Unger give an overview of the User-Centred Design lifecycle and the key methods and deliverables involved.

Optional reading for this module

  • Jill Butler, Kritina Holden, Will Lidwell - Universal Principles of Design: 100 Ways to Enhance Usability, Influence Perception, Increase Appeal, Make Better Design Decisions, and Teach Through Design
    This book is a useful reference for key design principles.
  • Krug, S. (2005) - Don't make me think! New Riders
    Short, easy-to-read and informative overview of web usability. A bit flippant about user testing at the end of the book, though.
  • Garrett, J.J. (2002) - The Elements of User Experience. New Riders.
    Garrett's text is an easy read, effectiely an extended description of the five-layer model he's famous for (see http://www.jjg.net/elements/pdf/elements.pdf
  • Rosenfeld, L. & Morville, P. (2002) - Information architecture for the world wide web. Second Edition. O'Reilly.
    The book on information architecture, also affectionately called the "Polar Bear Book". Quite detailed and lengthy, and interesting for those who want to further their understanding of enterprise information architecture. Make sure that you get the second edition - it's way more comprehensive and up-to-date than the first.
  • Alan Cooper, Robert Reimann, David Cronin - About Face 3: The Essentials of Interaction Design
    This all-in-one classic on interaction design, now in its third edition, is a great reference book and should be part of every user experience library.
  • Scott McCloud - Understanding Comics: The Invisible Art
    Not really a book about web design, or usability, but a great introduction to visual storytelling that will help you develop better storyboards.

Useful websites

  • Boxes and arrows - Forum for latest thinking in Information Architecture, Webdesign and Interaction Design
  • JJG.net - More IA resources.
  • UseIt - Jakob Nielsen's influential musings (and self-promotion!)
  • UXmatters http://www.uxmatters.com/
  • UX Magazine http://uxmag.com/

Further reading

  • Beyer, H. & Holtzblatt, K. (1998) - Contextual design Morgan Kauffman.
    Influential (and seriously detailed) book on contextual enquiry.
  • Brown, D (2006) - Communicating Design: Developing Web Site Documentation for Design and Planning. New Riders.
  • Cooper, A. (1999) - The inmates are running the asylum. Sams.
    The inventor of "personas" argues the case for user-centred design in software development.
  • Kuniavsky, M. (2003) - Observing the User Experience. Morgan Kaufman.
  • Moggridge, B. (2006) - Designing Interactions. MIT Press.
  • Nielsen, J. (2006) - Prioritizing Web Usability. New Riders.
  • Nielsen, J. (2000) - Designing web usability: the practive of simplicity. New Riders.
    It's Jakob. It's a classic.
  • Norman, D.A. (2005) - Emotional Design: Why We Love (or Hate) Everyday Things. Basic Books.
  • Saffer, D. (2006) - Designing for Interaction: Creating Smart Applications and Clever Devices. New Riders
  • Wodtke, C. (2002) - Information Architecture: Blueprints for the Web. New Riders.
