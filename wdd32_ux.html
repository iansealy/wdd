Web Design and Development

User Experience

What makes a good user experience?

Task 1

  • List 5 things that really annoy you about the web
  • List 5 things that you love about the web

Task 2

  • Think of two sites that you use regularly. Open them in your browser.
  • Analyse them in detail. Think carefully about what it is that makes them so attractive to you. Try to be as methodical and critical as you can.
  • Make a list of the good and bad aspects of these sites.

Content

Content is King! Content should:

  • be Fresh | High quality | Relevant | Engaging | Concise
  • Be created with the intended audience in mind
  • Be created with the presentation medium in mind
  • follow webwriting guidelines and good examples - the BBC

Information Design

  • Information should (usually) be presented clearly and unambiguously, whatever format it is presented in (text, images, diagrams)
  • Good website navigation is reliant on good information design
  • The presentation of information should generally conform to users expectations and 'mental models' - Intuitive

See: Edward Tufte (the acknowledged master of information design), and the BBC's election Swingometer (a classic!).

Performance

  • Web sites must be quick & responsive
  • Users hate to wait!
  • Don't forget that around 30% of users still have a 56K dialup connections

e.g. Coca-Cola

(Note: don't forget to optimise your graphics and use CSS whenever you can!)

Compatibility

  • Not being able to access content is the worst experience of all
  • Sites should be device-, platform- and browser-independent
  • Follow W3C standards for HTML, CSS & accessibility

Examples

  1. standards mean longevity for your site (unlike www.bornmagazine.org/projects/fuel/ when viewed with IE) and Quickbooks online.

Visual Design

  • Professional
  • High quality
  • Brand identity
  • Corporate identity

e.g. www.apple.com, www.lego.com

Interaction Design

  • What interface controls are available to a user? And how does the user "flow" through his task?
  • How does the system provide feedback & control for the user?
  • How are the controls made clear and intuitive?
  • Can the user recover from any errors?

Examples: Vodaphone future vision, Boohbah.com (kids interactive site), Rich Internet Applications (RIAs) - see Laszlo systems. And for the more revolutionary approach, what about Apple's new iPhone?
