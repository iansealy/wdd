Banner Animation

A banner, short for banner advertisement, is a graphic image/animation used for advertising on the Web. Some people find banner ads annoying but they are a very popular Web marketing tool.

Many sites sell space on their Web pages for banner ads. The aim of the banner ad is to lead people to a site. Most banner ads also link to the advertised site.

Example

Planning a Banner Animation

Find out as much as possible about the product/service that you are advertising. The more you know the easier the design process will be.

  • Decide on exactly what it is that the banner should accomplish.
  • Decide on what it needs to communicate.
  • What is the likely audience for the ad?
  • What does the page that the banner will appear in look like? Pay attention to the colour scheme and where it will fit on the page.
  • What size will the banner be? Banner sizes.

The Basics of Banner Design

To make a banner stand out use:

  • a contrasting colour scheme from the page in which it appears
  • a different typeface
  • motion/animation

Timing

Timing is important in your banner animation. Banner ads should usually last a maximum of 5 seconds. Some sites specify a 3 second 'loop'.

You also need to consider the timing of each frame. It is best to avoid 'stalling' on one particular frame for too long. The last frame, though, could be 'held' for slightly longer to create a pause before looping starts again.

Example

Text

Text in your banner should be kept to a minimum. Too much text overwhelms the viewer and he/she may not be able to keep up with the message if the animation is fast.

Example

Legibility

Make sure that any text that you include is readable. Be careful in your choice of background, typeface, and colour. Also consider the leading, kerning and font size.

Example

Text on Multiple Frames

One effective way of presenting text is to have it appear over multiple frames. With animation you can reveal a message gradually word by word or letter by letter.

Example

Looping

Looping can be an effective way of reinforcing a message but it can also be used to achieve other effects such as a continuous motion background.

Example

Blur

Blurred motion can be very effective in animation. It is possible to 'smooth out' the sometimes jerky motion of a GIF animation by using 'tweening' but you can also use a motion blur filter in Photoshop or ImageReady.

Example

File Size

There is an average limit to file size for banner animations of 15kb at 468x60 pixels. Keeping file size down to 15kb can be difficult but the folllowing tips may help.

  • Keep the background constant
  • Use as few colours as possible
  • Don't anti-alias text
