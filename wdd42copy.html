What is copyright?

Copyright is the exclusive legal right, given to an originator or assignee, for a fixed number of years to copy, adapt, distribute, lend or perform copyrighted material (and authorise others to do the same).

Copyright may be bought, sold, leased or waived.

What is covered? part 1

  • Literary works
  • Computer programs and databases
  • Dramatic works
  • Musical works
  • Artistic works
  • Computer generated works (e.g. fractals)
  • Sound recordings

What is covered? part 2

  • Films
  • Broadcasts
  • Cable programmes
  • Published editions
  • Rights of performers

Works can have several copyrights e.g. content, design, HTML code, CSS code.

Copyright clearing

Identifying the copyright owner:

  • You may already know who the owner is
  • Original author may not be the current copyright owner
  • Organisations - for example: DACS or CLA
  • Keep a Diligence File
  • The Enterprise and Regulatory Reform Act also introduces a new licensing scheme for 'orphan works' - works for which the copyright holder cannot be found after a 'diligent search'

Making contact with the Copyright holder

  • Making contact:
    • Ask once, but ask for everything
    • Get everything in writing
    • Record everything in the diligence file
  • Response:
    1. Permission denied
    2. They will consider it
    3. Permission granted
      • Do what you say
      • May have to pay

Making contact with the Copyright holder

  1. No response...
    • Give notice - offer to remove the work
    • Record everything in the diligence file
    • Be prepared to pay
    • Play safe and don't use the work - it's the only way to make sure you avoid an infringement claim

The more popular your site becomes the more likely it is that copyright infringements will be noticed. Don't think your infringement won't get noticed.

Fair dealing

Acts/usage that are permitted without infringing copyright.

Specified purposes:

  • Research or private study
  • Criticism or review
  • Accidental inclusion
  • Reporting current events (not including photographs)
  • Making content accessible to an individual

You need to prove "fair dealing".

Copyright

There is no such thing as copyright-free material - what exists is material for which the rights have been waived, certain use is permitted or for which the rights have expired.

Sources include the following but you must always read the 'Click-Wrap' agreement:

Copyright, Designs and Patents Act

  • Knowledge of Copyright, Designs and Patents Act (1998) may not be enough:-
    • Literary works are copyrighted for 70 years after the death of the originator
    • Sound recordings - 50 years from end of year of release or year of recording.
    • Databases, prior to 27 March 1996, treated as Literary Works. After 27 March 1996, the Database Right means that databases are covered for 15 years after creation - substantial updates can mean the term starts again so rolling protection can occur.
    • Remember, copyright terms can be extended.
  • Copyright law may be different in different countries.
    • In US, music can be copied to different formats.
    • In UK, "format shifting" was illegal until 1st October 2014

Can linking to a page be a Copyright infringement?

Well, possibly...

  • Linking directly to Copyrighted material may result in that material not being displayed as the Copyright holder intended (e.g. advertising).
  • Remember that copyright law exists to protect documents which cannot easily be protected by other technological means.
  • Contributory copyright infringement - if you "encourage" or provide a means by which others could breach copyright you could be liable (e.g. Napster in its original guise or circumventing pay walls).

Can linking to a page be a Copyright infringement? 2

  • Can provide text links instead of URLs
  • Vague authorship
  • Inline images/frames/co-branding

"Banning" linking breaks the fundamental architecture of the Web. If linking does not bypass advertising, payment or is expressly forbidden by the site then you should be ok.

Can linking to a site breach copyright?, Texas Court bans deep linking and some sites forbid it.

Sadly the request to add a right-to-link amendment to the Digital Economy Act was rejected.

Digital Economy Act

Despite the opposition, Bill passed during the wash-up before the General Election.

Specific issues:

  • the requirement that ISPs block access to sites that allow "substantial" infringement
  • "ISP" covers web hosts and domain registrars
  • Used to block access to The Pirate Bay

Protecting your copyright

Your work is automatically copyrighted.

Although it doesn't hurt to remind people:

  • Copyright © [date] [Author]
  • Using Copyright Notices
  • © sign (&copy;)
  • (C) is not recognised - and just tells everyone that you don't know the HTML entity ;-)

Protecting your copyright

  • Copyright stays with the author unless agreed in writing
  • Watermarking
  • Authentication
  • Decide what you will do if something is stolen
  • You need to be able to prove you're the originator of the work and often demonstrate loss of earnings or some other tangible impact
