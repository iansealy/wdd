What is Copyright?

Copyright is the exclusive legal right, given to an originator or assignee, for a fixed number of years to copy, adapt, distribute, lend or perform copyrighted material (and authorise others to do the same).

Copyright may be bought, sold, leased or waived.

What is covered?

  • Literary works
  • Computer programs and databases
  • Dramatic works
  • Musical works
  • Artistic works
  • Computer generated works
  • Sound recordings

What is covered?

  • Films
  • Broadcasts
  • Cable programmes
  • Published editions
  • Rights of performers

Works can have several copyrights e.g. content, design, CSS code.

Copyright clearing

  • Original author may not be the current owner
  • Ask once, but ask for everything
  • Get everything in writing
  • Keep a diligence file

Identifying the copyright owner:

  • You may already know this
  • Organisations - for example: DACS or CLA
  • Write everything down in your diligence file

Making contact with the Copyright holder

  1. Permission denied
  2. They will consider it
  3. Permission granted
    • Do what you say

Making contact with the Copyright holder

  1. No response
    • Give notice - offer to remove the work
    • Diligence file
    • Be prepared to pay
    • Play safe and don't use the work - it's the only way to make sure you avoid an infringement claim.
    • Use the Copyright Tribunal

The more popular your site becomes the more likely it is that copyright infringements will be noticed. Don't think your infringement won't be noticed.

Fair Use/Dealing

"An individual may make a single copy of all or a substantial part of a copyrighted item so long as it is 'fair' and it is for one of the specified purposes"

Specified purposes:

  • Research or private study
  • Criticism or review
  • Reporting current events

You need to prove "fair use".

Copyright

There is no such thing as copyright-free material - what exists is material which the rights have been waived, certain use is permitted or for which the rights have expired. For example:

  • WikiMedia Commons
  • stock.xchng
  • A - Z of Clip-art
  • Free Fonts

Make sure you read the 'Click-Wrap' agreement.

Copyright Act

Knowledge of the Copyright, Designs and Patents Act (1998) may not be enough. Copyright can be extended.

Literary works are copyrighted for 70 years after the death of the originator (without extension this work could then be used without seeking permission).

Sound recordings - 50 years from end of year of release or year of recording.

Databases = 15 years (but substantial updates can mean the term starts again so rolling protection can occur)

Copyright law may be different in different countries (In US, music can be copied to different formats. In UK this is still illegal. New proposals outlined in January 2008 propose relaxation but not circumventing of DRM.)

Copyright Act 2

Copyright law can be applied retrospectively.

Can linking to a page be a Copyright infringement?

Well, possibly...

  • Linking directly to Copyrighted material may result in that material not being displayed as the Copyright holder intended (e.g. advertising)
    - Remember that copyright law exists to protect documents which cannot easily be protected by other technological means.
  • Contributory copyright infringement - if you "encourage" or provide a means by which others could breach copyright you could be liable (e.g. Napster in its original guise).

Can linking to a page be a Copyright infringement?

  • Can provide text links instead of URLs
  • Vague authorship
  • Inline images/frames/co-branding

"Banning" linking breaks the fundamental architecture of the Web. If linking does not bypass advertising, payment or is expressly forbidden by the site then you should be ok.
