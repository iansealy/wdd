Business Analysis

  • It's vital to understand the business goals, success factors and politics of the organisation you are working in.

Define project criteria

Scope & definitions

  • WHAT is your site supposed to do?

Primary purpose / goal of project

  • WHY are you doing it? (what are the business benefits?)

Business model

Revenue generating

  • Commerce (e.g. Argos)
  • Advertising (e.g. MySpace)
  • Subscription (e.g Nature)

Non-revenue generating?

  • promotional (e.g. BP)
  • cost-saving (e.g. Microsoft support)

Measures of success

HOW will you assess success? What "metrics" will you use?

A client survey is an essential tool at this stage (see task).

Formulate strategies

  • What resources are available? (people, equipment etc...)
  • What methods / processes will you adopt?
  • What is your schedule?
  • What is your budget?

(covered in more detail in WDD31)

Understand the context in which you are working

  • What are the politics of project? (Don't underestimate the importance of politics!)
  • Who are the stakeholders? (WHO has a vested interest in this project?)
  • What decision structures are there? (WHERE does the power lie?)

Build relationships

  • Get to know people. Have one-to-one meetings.
  • Listen to them. Don't spend the time telling them about all your great ideas!
  • When you need to, communicate your ideas clearly and effectively.
  • Establish common frames of reference between stakeholders.
  • Identify conflicts.

Benefits of Business Analysis

  • It clarifies thinking (i.e. it's results focused)
  • It reduces the need to make too many assumptions (since you should have discussed things!)
  • It confirms pre-stated requirements
  • It informs and supports the design team
  • Helps to define project success from the business perspective
