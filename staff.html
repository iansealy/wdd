Staff

Tim Priestman, Course Director
Tim Priestman joined one of the first interactive media companies in the UK as a designer in 1983. He worked in new media companies for 15 years in design, sales and marketing, project management and in consultancy. Clients have included Philips, Marks & Spencer, HM Customs and Excise and PPP Healthcare. He has lectured on this Postgraduate Certificate and on the Postgraduate Certificate in New Media Management at Birkbeck College since 1995.

Ian Sealy, Lecturer
Ian Sealy has been developing Web sites since 1995. From 1997 until 2004 he worked for the Institute for Learning and Research Technology, an international centre of excellence based at the University of Bristol. His work as a Senior Technical Researcher within the Internet Development group involved developing Web sites for a wide variety of clients, both commercial (for example, Ford) and educational (for example, the National Maritime Museum). From 2004 until 2007 he worked as a Senior Computer Biologist at the Wellcome Trust Sanger Institute before leaving to become Head of Technology at The Local Europe AB, a European news organisation. In 2010 he returned to the Wellcome Trust Sanger Institute as a Senior Bioinformatician working on the Zebrafish Mutation Project.

Jonathan Fox, Lecturer
Jonathan Fox studied graphic design and photography at Maidstone College of Art and the Polytechnic of Central London and gained an MA in History and Theory of Modern Art at Chelsea College of Art and Design. Currently teaching part-time at Camberwell College of Arts and Goldsmiths College and working as a freelance Web designer.

Kieren Pitts, Lecturer
Kieren Pitts began developing Web sites in 1995 and is an experienced developer, having worked in the education, charity and commercial sectors and also as a freelancer. Kieren is currently employed as a Senior Technical Researcher by Research IT, the research and development division of the University of Bristol's IT Services. Kieren's primary role is lead developer on Bristol Online Surveys (BOS) - an online survey tool used by approximately 300 institutions and with more than 36,000 registered users. His main interests are in Web standards, accessibility, programming (Python, Perl and PHP), application security and games development.

Peter Otto, Lecturer
Peter Otto is Design Director at Condeco, a specialist software provider. Before that he worked at Morgan Stanley, where he designed user interfaces for Morgan Stanley's financial trading software, and at Flow, a London consultancy for user research and user-centred design, where he helped clients such as Sony, Easyjet, BT and Standard Life develop customer-centric online experiences. Peter's experience covers the disciplines of user research, design strategy & innovation, interaction design and information architecture.

Inês Rebelo, Lecturer
Inês Rebelo completed a Masters in Fine Art at Goldsmiths, University of London and a PGCE (PC) at the Institute of Education. She has over five years of experience in IT services in higher education and is currently a member of the Web Design team at Goldsmiths. Highlights in her visual arts practice include several awards in the UK and abroad.

Martina Schell, Lecturer
Martina is a User Experience consultant specialising in user centered design (UCD), experience strategy and qualitative design research. She has over fifteen years experience in interactivity for web, desktop, TV and mobile devices, with a background in brand development. She co-founded UX Tuesday and mentors at Seedcamp & Method Design Lab to bring UX expertise to start-ups. Martina serves on the User Experience Practitioners' Association (UXPA) committee, and regularly hosts, and speaks at, industry events.
