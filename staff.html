Staff

Tim Priestman, Course Director
Tim Priestman joined one of the first interactive media companies in the UK as a designer in 1983. He worked in new media companies for 15 years in design, sales and marketing, project management and in consultancy. Clients have included Philips, Marks & Spencer, HM Customs and Excise and PPP Healthcare. He has lectured on this Diploma and on the Diploma in New Media Management at Birkbeck College since 1995.

Ian Sealy, Lecturer
Ian Sealy has been developing Web sites since 1995. From 1997 until 2004 he worked for the Institute for Learning and Research Technology, an international centre of excellence based at the University of Bristol. His work as a Senior Technical Researcher within the Internet Development group involved developing Web sites for a wide variety of clients, both commercial (for example, Ford) and educational (for example, the National Maritime Museum). He is currently a Senior Computer Biologist at the Wellcome Trust Sanger Institute.

Jonathan Fox, Lecturer
Jonathan Fox studied graphic design and photography at Maidstone College of Art and the Polytechnic of Central London and gained an MA in History and Theory of Modern Art at Chelsea College of Art and Design. Currently teaching part-time at Camberwell College of Arts and Goldsmiths College and working as a freelance Web designer.

Kieren Pitts, Lecturer
Kieren Pitts has been developing Web sites since 1995 and has gained commercial experience working as a Web developer within the scientific marketing and PR sector. Kieren is currently employed as a Senior Technical Researcher by the Institute for Learning and Research Technology, University of Bristol. In recent years Kieren's research interests have centred on the development of interactive and educational online games and simulations. He also has significant freelance experience in commercial and educational sectors.

Stuart Church, Lecturer
Stuart Church is director of Pure Usability Limited, a Web usability and accessibility consultancy. He previously worked as an Information Architect for Cambridge Training and Development Ltd and the University of Cambridge. Prior to that, he was an academic at the University of Bristol and has been involved in the design and implementation of Web sites within Higher Education since 1994.

Darius Kumana, Lecturer
Darius Kumana's technical grounding through a degree in computer science was extended by commercial experience in both business analysis and graphic design. Darius's experience ranges from small freelance projects to leading teams within high profile commercial Web projects with budgets of several million pounds. Darius is currently Head of New Media at Cambridge Assessment (University of Cambridge) where he has been tasked with building a centre of excellence for user-centred design.
