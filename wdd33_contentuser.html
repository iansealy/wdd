Let the design begin!

We know about the big goals of the site, what users want to achieve, and what the content is (roughly) going to be. We now have to start working towards a design for our site.

There is no magic formula for archieving this!

Where do we start?

The IA has a number of different tools and approaches to guide them

  • Knowledge of user tasks - always put this at the forefront of design
  • Use existing Web patterns - convention is very powerful
  • Inspiration - maybe there are better ways of doing things
  • Use of real-world metaphors - take advantage of the fact that people already have an understanding of the way certain things work in the real-world
  • Organize and label content based on user mental models

Bringing content and user tasks together

  • Ideally, we need content audit (an inventory of all content available for the site)
  • Identify key tasks and user mental models from user stories and task analysis (broken down into simple steps)
  • Associate content and functionality with user's mental models
  • Content should also be written to address the needs of the target users
  • Identify redundant content

Jeff Veen has a great set of notes on The Frontier of User Experience (WARNING: 5MB PDF file!) that gives a nice cartoon overview of mental models
