Producing great content

Keep pages relatively short in length

  • Try to split up long pages (but provide printable versions of the full text), e.g. WebMonkey
  • It's 25% slower to read from screen than paper
  • Users don't usually like to scroll much. And they HATE to scroll horizontally!! e.g. Horizontal scrolling must die!
  • Avoid irrelevant content (e.g. Welcome to our site!) and splash pages, e.g. Man Utd

Make sure the quality of the content is high

  • NO spelling errors or typos - use a spellchecker (SHIFT + F7 in Dreamweaver)
  • GOOD grammar
  • Provide well-written, engaging, text (be creative!)
  • Avoid jargon & acronyms, e.g. Hummmingbird
  • Follow a style guide if possible e.g. webstyleguide.com, Apple's style guide(PDF, 870K)

Make pages scannable

  • Bullet points are useful
  • Use meaningful headlines
  • Highlighting and emphasis where appropriate
  • Good visual design of text (visual hierarchy), e.g. Boxes and arrows versus Useit
  • "inverted pyramid" style of writing, e.g. BBC

Legibility

  • Use plain backgrounds, e.g. Circlemakers
  • Don't write in uppercase - IT'S VERY DIFFICULT TO READ FROM A SCREEN ANYWAY. PUTTING EVERYTHING IN UPPERCASE MAKES IT EVEN HARDER BECAUSE IT REDUCES THE SPACING BETWEEN LETTERS. IT ALSO MAKES IT SOUND LIKE YOU ARE SHOUTING ALL THE TIME. SO THE BEST THING TO DO IS AVOID IT UNLESS YOU REALLY NEED TO.

Task

Take a look at a passage of text from a real website. Is this an appropriate style of writing for the web? What would you do to improve it if you could?

An aside: Auditing existing content

You can't design a site until you know what the content will be, so you need to carry out a content audit. This is basically a list of every single file on the site (HTML, images, Flash, Word, PDF, Powerpoint etc.)

Luckily, you can often do this automatically using a tool such as Xenu Link Sleuth
