Personas & Scenarios : bringing your users to life

We need a process whereby:

  • Users are kept at the forefront of the design process
  • Information about users is readily communicated to different stakeholders
  • Information about users and their goals is memorable

Personas and Scenarios are a means by which this may be achieved.

Personas

  • Devised by Alan Cooper (www.cooper.com)
  • A persona is a fictitious person for whom you are designing the website
  • The persona should be a stereotype of one of your audience types
  • It provides a humanising focus for development; you are able to talk about "Derek", rather than "the user"
  • The persona reflects REAL things about your site users; persona development is informed by user research

Constructing a persona

Constructing a persona isn't simply a case of making it up as you go along! Spend time creating personas that really represent your users. The better your personas are, the better your design will be.

  • Include information from audience / user analysis (e.g. demographics, motivation, Internet experience, habits, favorite sites)
  • Pull memorable quotes from your interviews into personas
  • Give a persona a name, and even have a photo of them (from a photo library)
  • It is common to construct 4 to 5 personas in order to cover a range of user types
  • It is helpful to have a single primary persona who is considered to represent the most important user group for your site
  • Useful for bringing user-centred design into an organization and keeping it at the forefront of development

What happens with the personas once they've been created?

They're a communication tool - share them!

  • Keep them nearby - pin them on the wall!
  • Share them with other stakeholders.

Task: Surrey Redesign - Part1

Imagine that you are involved in a redesign of www.surrey.ac.uk. We're going to try to reverse engineer the user types for this site.

  • Who are your users?
  • How many different types of users might there be?
  • What sort of characteristics might they have?
  • Create a detailed primary persona for this site.

Scenarios

Personas lead quite naturally into the development of Scenarios. Once users have been identified, their goals need to be articulated.

There are lots of different scenario related terms and techniques (scenarios, use-cases, user-stories etc) - but all should focus on user goals.

Scenarios 2

An example approach to Scenarios:

  • In the form: As a [user] I want/need [function] so that [rational]
  • Formulated as one or two sentences in the everyday language of the user
  • Keep them short - use of cards.
  • As high level or detailed as appropriate

The Power of Scenarios

Scenarios are useful for:

  • Requirements specification
  • Communicating goals and ensuring a common understanding across teams
  • Scoping
  • Acceptance & Success Testing
  • The solution is not always a website!
